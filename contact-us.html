Learn some important vocabulary for writing



DEAR

Dear

Used as an affectionate or friendly form of address.
For example:
Dear [Name],
Dear Mr./Ms./Dr./Professor [Last name],

MR

Mr.

A title used before the family name or full name of a man who has no other title, or when talking to man who holds a particular official position.
For example:
Mr Jones/Mr David Jones.

MISS

Miss.

A title of respect for a female child and an unmarried woman.
For example:
Miss Smith/ Miss García

Mrs.

Mrs.

A title used before a surname or full name to address or refer to a married woman.
For example:
Mrs Smith/ Miss García

Moreover

Moreover

In addition to what has been said.
For example:
I liked this book because it was well-written and, moreover, told a very interesting story.

On the other hand

On the other hand.

Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with a previous statement.
For example:
He's a good guy. His brother, on the other hand, is a very selfish man.

Though

Though

Despite the fact that; although.
For example:
I like him. Though he makes me angry sometimes.

Therefore

Therefore

For that reason.


For example:

We were working while she performed her new song in live. Therefore, we had to watch it on youtube.
